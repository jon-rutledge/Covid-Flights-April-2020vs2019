Flight Data Explorative Analysis - Coronavirus¶

Preliminary Wrangling¶

This year we saw the advent of the Coronavirus outbreak and pandemic. As of July, new daily cases are still rising here in the United States and travel restrictions, whether they be government mandated or self imposed by the population, are changing the demand for airline travel.

Note about data Collected¶

The interface on the Bureau of Transportation and Statistics allows for users to manually download sets of flight data from its website. For this analysis we will be sticking with 2 months worth of data, specifically the periods of April 2019 and April 2020. I wish to see if there are any significant trends in Airline usage or performance during the Cronovirus pandemic.

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

This dataset is a single table that is composed of individual flight statistics reported to the Bureau of Transportation of the United States. This includes attributes such as time of take of and landing, point of origin, delay, etc.

Structure is 925,405 observations with 35 separate attributes. 1 extra column will be removed as it is unnamed. There are many numeric based values related to time or duration. There are also categorical values related to identifiying point of origin, destination, flight, and carrier.

More details on columns and their values can be found HERE

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

In this dataset, I am primarily interested to see if the Coronavirus pandemic significantly affected flight trafic performance and quantity. This will mostly be tied to operational efficiency and delays.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

Starting off, roughly count will be a good high level indicator. After that moving into specifics for certain states that may have been hit harder. It is also possible, that if there are less people traveling, there will be less delays caused by passengers, such as security issues.

Ok first let's discuss positive correlations. Kind of self explanatory but departure and arrival delays are heavily correlated. We may want to take a look at this later on.

For negative correlations, we can see the affect of the overall decrease observed earlier between YEAR and DAY_OF_MONTH. Interestingly, there is also a negative correlation between delay values and the year. Maybe the decrease of volume has eased up on general pressure allowing flights to operate more smoothly.
